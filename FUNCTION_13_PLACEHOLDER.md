# Function 13: Intentionally Left Blank

## Why No Function 13?

**Triskaidekaphobia** - the fear of the number 13 - is one of the most common superstitions across many cultures worldwide.

### Cultural Context

- **Buildings**: Many skyscrapers skip the 13th floor, jumping from 12 directly to 14
- **Airlines**: Row 13 is frequently omitted from seating charts
- **Street Addresses**: Some cities avoid using "13" in addresses
- **Friday the 13th**: Considered an especially unlucky day
- **Apollo 13**: The only unsuccessful lunar mission had this number

### Historical Origins

The superstition surrounding 13 has various possible origins:
- **The Last Supper**: 13 people were present before Jesus's crucifixion
- **Norse Mythology**: The god Loki was the 13th guest at a dinner, leading to chaos
- **Numerology**: 12 is considered a "complete" number (12 months, 12 zodiac signs, 12 hours), so 13 disrupts that completeness

### Our Decision

In the spirit of tradition (and perhaps a bit of whimsy), we've chosen to skip Function 13 in CABB.

**Why tempt fate when manipulating bibliographic records?**

The function sequence goes:
- **Function 11a**: Prepare TIFF/JPG Representations
- **Function 11b**: Upload JPG Files
- **Function 12**: Analyze Sound Records by Decade
- **Function 13**: ⊘ (You are here - intentionally blank)
- **Function 14a**: Prepare Thumbnails

## Philosophy

Sometimes it's okay to embrace a bit of superstition, especially when:
- It doesn't hurt anyone
- It makes the software more interesting
- It respects cultural traditions
- It gives users a smile

**After all, library and archival work is serious enough without adding unlucky numbers to the mix!**
